FARMING

Farming in Puspus: Growing Roots, Sustaining Lives

Barangay Puspus is a community deeply rooted in agriculture, where most families depend on farming as their main source of income. The fertile lands of the barangay nurture a variety of crops, including cacao, vegetables, and other local produce that sustain both livelihood and tradition. Farming is not just a way of living here — it is a reflection of the residents’ hard work, dedication, and connection to the land. Through their agricultural practices, the people of Barangay Puspus continue to cultivate growth, resilience, and pride in their community, turning every harvest into a symbol of hope and progress.

What You Get

Pasca’s Farm

It showcases the rich farming culture of Barangay Puspus. From March to April, golden yellow corn is harvested at ₱17/kg, followed by palay from May to December at ₱15–₱20/kg. A year-round symbol of hard work and abundance.

Alfonso Galvez’s Farm

a legacy of hard work passed down through generations. For decades, this family farm has focused on cultivating rice (₱17–₱18/kg) and corn (₱20/kg), sustaining both livelihood and tradition. A true testament to the enduring spirit of farming in Barangay Puspus.
